The Invention of the Internet

The inception of the internet was no small feat; it required the effort of many over a course of decades to get to where it is today.

Advanced Research Projects Agency
Year - 1957

President Eisenhower created the Advance Research Projects Agency (ARPA) which was in charge of the Space Program and Computer Development. Eventually NASA took over the space program and ARPA became its own entity.

Queueing Theory
Year - 1962

Leonard Kleinrock a PHD student at MIT for his thesis proposed the Queueing Theory which was the basis for the packet switching which would be used for computer networks later on in the decade.

Circuit Theory and Soviet Union
Year - 1962

Circuit Switching is a technology used where the circuit is first established between two point and then a message could be sent between those two points. However the US government realized that if the Soviet union would ever attack them it could completely bring down the telecommunication infrastructure at that time because the connection between two points would not be established. Thus the government pushed ARPA to come up with a solution to this problem, thus accelerating computer research.
The Need for Interconnected Networks
Year - 1966

Bob Taylor an employee at ARPA was using terminals to access computers; and realized that it was annoying to use a different terminal per computer and theorized that a single terminal could suffice to talk to multiple computers at once. He wanted Heterogenous computers to communicate with one another across a network; he pitched the idea to his higher ups and received 1 million dollars in funding to do so.

Packet Switching and Lawrence Roberts
Year - 1968

Bob Taylor hired Lawrence Roberts an MIT graduate to help build the ARPA computer network. They came up with something called Packet Switching which is when a message is cut into bits and transmitted in a network and finds its way to the destination by itself and the receiving end reconstructs the message. The issue then being that each computer did not know how to talk to the other computers due to a language barrier.
Integrated Message Processors (IMP)
Year - 1969

Interface Message Processors were developed, they were a type of translator for each computer so that they could ‘speak’ one unified language and communicate with one another, and a company called BBN won the contract to produce them. UCLA wrote code to allow the I.M.P to communicate with one another. By the end of 1969 the I.M.P arrived at UCLA and 4 computers were on the network.

ARPANET
Year - 1971

Two years since the first network was established ARPANET had 18 mainframe computers connected to the network. A ‘killer’ application was also launched in 1971, Email. Ray Tomlinson of ARPA made the application and allowed people to communicate much quicker within the network, came the use of the ‘@’ symbol
LAN and WAN
Year - 1972

ARPA had fewer than 25 sites online in the ARPANET and demonstrated it in Washington DC and it was huge hit. More and more places started using this technology to created their own networks for their offices or campuses and there came Local Area Networks and Wide Area Networks, the former (LAN) being devices connected to each other in a network and the latter (WAN) being those networks connected together in a larger network.

TCP/IP Protocols
Year - 1972

Vinton Cerf and Robert E Kahn were the inventors of TCP/IP protocols these protocols allowed computer networks to connect with another with the use of a gateway, thus ushering in a universal standard for interconnected networks
World Wide Web
Year - 1989

Tim-Berners Lee invented the World Wide Web which with the use of hyperlinks and HTTPS allowed users to follow a link from one website to another.

Access to the Net
Year - 1992

The internet grew by word of mouth and more advancements in computing technology like the mouse; desktop pc and the high-speed modem. An act of congress took internet out of government and made it available to the public. One drawback being that it was difficult to use the internet if you were not well versed with computers.

Internet Revolution and Adoption
Year - 1993

Mosaic was invented and later went to become Netscape a browser that allowed users to get graphically rich content in an easy to use; point and click format. Due to this advancement the internet has 341,000% growth.
